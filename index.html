HOW TO MAKE MY DELICIOUS NIGERIAN JOLLOF

A TASTY NIGERIAN DISH

DESCRIPTION OF THE DISH

Nigerian Jollof rice is a vibrant, flavorful West African dish known for its rich, smoky, and spicy taste. It's a one-pot rice dish, traditionally cooked over firewood, which imparts a distinctive smoky flavor, although it can also be made on the stovetop. The dish features long-grain rice cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce with a blend of spices, including onions, peppers (like scotch bonnet for heat), and other aromatic herbs. It's often served with fried plantains, meat (like chicken, beef, or goat), or other accompaniments.

HELPFUL TIPS TO A GOOD JOLLOF RICE

  • Choose the Right Pot. The type of pot you use can make or break your jollof rice.
  • Control the Heat. Cooking jollof rice on high heat can increase burning.
  • Use the Right Amount of Water.
  • Stir gently.
  • Don't Overload the Pot, use a much bigger pot if available.
  • Use Fresh Ingredients.
  • Avoid local Nigerian rice.

Ingredients

  • 5 cups grain parboiled rice
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • Scotch Bonnet pepper (ATA RODO)
  • 3 tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons gino tomato paste
  • Chicken
  • Ponmo
  • Any preferable meat of your choice
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt, pepper, and maggi to taste
  • Habanero (optional for spice)
  • Cooked chicken or beef (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice in water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine tomatoes and scotch bonnet. Blend until smooth.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Slice onions to smaller sizes.
  4. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes. Then add the blended mixture and cook until it reduces (about 10–15 minutes).
  5. Add thyme, curry, bay leaf, and seasoning. Stir well.
  6. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil. Add rice and reduce heat to low.
  7. Cover and simmer for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  8. Once the rice is soft and the liquid is absorbed, turn off the heat and let it steam for 5–10 minutes.
  9. Serve with plantains, salad, or protein of choice.
